Anna-May Robey On Love Island 2023

One candidate from Swansea will be on the ITV show Love Island when it returns to our screens on Monday. With nine other hopefuls, Anna-May Robey is joining the South African villa. The program smashed records last summer by being the most-watched series on the ITV Hub, with more than 269 million views, and is currently airing its ninth series on ITV2 and for the first time on ITVX.

About why she joined the show, Anna explained, “I’ve been single for quite a while my mother is bugging me, she’s like, ‘You need to find a boyfriend.’ I’m just excited to try something new and I think going on Love Island is a good way to meet someone.”

Anna described herself as “funny, energetic and spontaneous” and will bring lots of fun to the villa. “I’ll bring energy to the Villa, I’ll get everyone up playing games!”, she told ITV2.

Talking about her icks, she explained, “Watching a boy get his haircut and the barber pushing up the seat, you know the pumping thing? I used to go to the barber with my ex and I used to sit there and every time the barber would do it, I would have to look away.”

Anna-May’s dream dinner would have guests like Anthony Joshua, Ricky Gervais, and Olly Murs, while Coldplay would make up the soundtrack. “I love Coldplay, I absolutely love them, I’d probably listen to Coldplay forever,” she says.

Laura Whitmore will be replaced by Maya Jama as the show’s new host in the 2023 season. ITV made the announcement that this year’s candidates will have to suspend their social media accounts last month in order to safeguard themselves, their families, and the cause of good mental health.

The broadcaster will invite islanders to make their accounts “dormant” for the first time while they are in the villa so that nothing may be published on their behalf and so they can prevent “the negative impacts of social media.” After the most recent series generated thousands of complaints to media watchdog Ofcom, they are also receiving “advice and training” about “mutually respectful behavior in relationships.”
